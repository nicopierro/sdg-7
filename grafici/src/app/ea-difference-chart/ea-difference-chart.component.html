2010 vs 2019

In 2019, the global electricity access rate reached 90 per cent, which is a significant improvement from 2010, when it was only 83 per cent. However, there will still be over 660 million people without electricity in 2030. The COVID-19 pandemic is threatening the progress that has been made in reducing greenhouse gas emissions and providing electricity to millions of people and the progress made in many countries in providing basic electricity services. As a result, lower oil and gas prices are threatening to deter investors from acquiring clean energy technologies. In 2020, the number of people without access to electricity in Africa increased by almost half. This is due to the increasing levels of poverty and population growth in developing nations.
